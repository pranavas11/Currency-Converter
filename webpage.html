Currency 1 Information

The United States dollar (USD) is the official currency of the United States and several other countries. The Coinage Act of 1792 introduced the U.S. dollar. The U.S. dollar was originally defined under a bimetallic standard of 371.25 grains (24.057 g) fine silver or, from 1837, 23.22 grains (1.505 g) fine gold. The Gold Standard Act of 1900 linked the dollar solely to gold. From 1934, its equivalence to gold was revised to $35 per troy ounce. Since 1971 all links to gold have been repealed.
